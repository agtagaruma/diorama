Diorama

The Power of Literacy in Shaping Our World

"From Written Records to Modern Technology: Exploring the Link Between Literacy and Civilization through Design"

View Project Larger

As a designer, the idea of creating a diorama or simulation that shows how Baguio City has changed over time intrigues me. A potent visual representation demonstrating the evolution of civilizations also embodies the notion that our world today has been shaped by centuries of toil and progress.


As I work on this project, my thoughts frequently turn to the importance of literacy and its influence on our civilization. From the earliest written records to current technology, literacy has been a major force in fostering communication and advancement.


In my opinion, literacy is a key that unlocks the door to understanding and knowing things, but critical thinking abilities and creative thinking are equally important. Certain skills are essential for navigating through modern life's many complexities, and people looking to succeed in our global economy are increasingly in demand for them.


The making of my diorama has taught me how inextricably linked the modernization of literature and civilization are, and where we are now is depicted by the border of an hourglass, reminding us that our world is always changing and progressing.


At the core of it all is the basic reality that literacy is the way to better our culture. Whether I'm constructing a busy downtown or a modest ancient village, I'm reminded of the power of human ingenuity and imagination, as well as the crucial role that literacy has had in shaping our world.

Next, go to the SketchUp Web App in your web browser.


Once the SketchUp web application is loaded, click on the "Open from Device" button.

Click on the "Open" button to start loading the SketchUp model file.


Wait for the SketchUp model file to load completely. This may take some time due to the complexity of the model.


Once the SketchUp model file is loaded, you can navigate the 3D perspective using the "O" key on your keyboard.
